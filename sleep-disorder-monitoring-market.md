<p><p>The sleep disorder monitoring market is categorized into four types based on the complexity of monitoring capabilities. Type I monitors offer comprehensive polysomnography in a sleep laboratory setting. Type II monitors also provide polysomnography but can be used in home settings. Type III monitors are designed for portable recording and focus primarily on limited parameters like oxygen saturation and airflow. Type IV monitors are the simplest, capturing only minimal data, typically focusing on single vital signs for basic sleep assessment.</p></p>
