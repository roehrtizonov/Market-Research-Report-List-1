<p><p>The laser resurfacing machines market is divided into two primary types: ablative and nonablative lasers. Ablative lasers remove the outer layers of skin, promoting collagen production and effectively treating wrinkles, scars, and sun damage. In contrast, nonablative lasers penetrate the skin without removing layers, stimulating collagen growth while causing less downtime and discomfort. Both types are used for skin rejuvenation, but their approaches and recovery times differ, catering to varied patient needs and preferences in cosmetic dermatology.</p></p>
