<p><p>The MMR vaccine market consists of several types: monovalent, combinational, divalent, and trivalent vaccines. Monovalent vaccines target a single virus, focusing solely on measles, mumps, or rubella. Combinational vaccines encompass multiple antigens in one dosage, such as MMRV, which includes varicella. Divalent vaccines protect against two viruses, while trivalent vaccines, like the standard MMR, safeguard against all three diseases—measles, mumps, and rubella—simultaneously, enhancing public health by streamlining immunization efforts.</p></p>
